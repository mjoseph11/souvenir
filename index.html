Life & Ministry

Born on July 3, 1942 in Mallassery, Pathanamthitta, Kerala, India, Mathai Achen felt the call to ministry from a young age — beginning to preach at just eight years old.

He was ordained a deacon in 1974 by H.G. Dr. Mathews Mar Athanasios (then Catholicate-designate) at St. Mary's Orthodox Church, Lakkoor, Kerala. On April 14, 1975, his marriage to Mariamma Abraham was solemnized by Athanasios Thirumeni; two weeks later, on April 28, 1975, the same Thirumeni elevated him to the holy priesthood in Kerala. The following year, he moved from New York to Philadelphia to plant the first Malankara Orthodox parish in the region.

In 1976, he established what began as St. Thomas Indian Orthodox Church, celebrating the first liturgy at the Church of the Savior Cathedral chapel, 38th and Chestnut Streets, Philadelphia. In 1984, the parish was reorganized as St. Gregorios Malankara Orthodox Church. In 1996, H.G. Mathews Mar Barnabas Metropolitan elevated him to Corepiscopos in Philadelphia.

Over 35 years of faithful service, he guided the parish through its growth, relocation, and the consecration of its permanent home at 4136 Hulmeville Road, Bensalem on Christmas Day, 2008.

The Beginnings

In the Words of Rev. Fr. Dr. Paul Varki • Co-Founder

Rev. Fr. Dr. Paul Varki

Co-Founder • St. Gregorios Malankara Orthodox Church

A first-person account of the parish's earliest days, written by the deacon who hosted Philadelphia's pioneering ecumenical Orthodox worship and petitioned the Catholicos for the first parish kalpana.

In the summer of 1965, some of us who were working and studying in Philadelphia and surrounding areas got together and planned a worship service of different denominations, including the Malankara Orthodox Church. Fr. Dr. K. M. Simon and Fr. A. T. Abraham were early celebrants of the Orthodox Holy Qurbana, and I was the deacon who assisted and hosted those priests. In addition, the late H. G. Mar Makarios (then Fr. K. C. Thomas), the late H. G. Mar Theodosious (then Fr. P. T. Punnoose, who was my dear friend from my old seminary days), the late Rev. Fr. John Mathews, Very Rev. Fr. Cherian Neelankal, and Very Rev. Fr. Yohannan Sankarathil were some of the other priests who conducted the Holy Qurbana in the beginning.

The Orthodox Qurbana was conducted once a month at Asbury Church, which was given to us rent-free. In those days we had Marthoma, Catholic, CSI, and other denominational members who attended the services. These services were opened to all. Thus, in Philadelphia — the City of Brotherly Love — the vineyard which St. Thomas, our founding father and disciple of Jesus, started in Odessa, was born.

After each Sunday service we got together in someone's residence for lunch and fellowship. This went on for eleven years. After awhile, some groups separated and started their own worship service in the seventies. I contacted Bishop Ogylby of the Episcopal Church, who [gave us his] Cathedral and chapel rent-free for Qurbana [at] 38th and Chestnut Streets. We also started our own service and Qurbana, which Rev. Fr. K. Mathai (now V. Rev. Fr. K. Mathai) kindly conducted. The first Diocesan Bishop, H. G. Mar Makarios — founder of the American Diocese — appointed him as Vicar in 1979.

Before Mathai Achen came to Philadelphia as Vicar, I sent an application to the Catholicos H. H. Baselios Marthoma Mathews I (who was not only my teacher at M. D. Seminary, but my benefactor), and His Holiness sent me a kalpana authorizing a parish in Philadelphia. It was on the basis of that kalpana of authorization that Mathai Achen was appointed Vicar by H. G. Mar Makarios, and the kalpana was read by Very Rev. Fr. K. Mathai in the church. After H. G. Mar Makarios came and inaugurated the American Diocese, the St. Thomas Orthodox Church — the first Orthodox church in Philadelphia — came into being. This is now St. Gregorios.

The beginnings still go through the currents of our souls — the earliest settlers in Philadelphia. May the patron Saint, Mar Gregorios, whose memory we cherish, bless this elegant church in Bensalem, PA, which is the outcome of the dedicated and courageous people of our beloved church.

Our Story

Our community traces its origins to 1962, when Fr. K. M. Simon celebrated the first Malayalam service in Dr. T. V. Philip's apartment on Pine Street, Philadelphia. After fourteen years of ecumenical Sunday worship, the parish was officially founded in October 1976 by V. Rev. Kaleeckal Mathai Cor Episcopos — first as St. Thomas Indian Orthodox Church, with the Holy Qurbana celebrated at the Church of the Savior Cathedral chapel, 38th & Chestnut Streets.

In 1984, following parish reorganization, our community was reconstituted as St. Gregorios Malankara Orthodox Church — in honor of St. Geevarghese Gregorios of Parumala, the first Indian saint of the Orthodox Church. Fr. K. Mathai continued as Vicar.

A 1994 attempt to build on Verree Road was prevented by zoning. In 1995 the parish purchased its first permanent home at 7721 Torresdale Avenue, Philadelphia. Land at 4136 Hulmeville Road, Bensalem was acquired in 2005 (with adjacent property added in 2006); the new church was first occupied on Christmas Day 2008 and consecrated May 22–23, 2009.

In 2011, our parish hosted the historic Metropolitan Enthronement of His Grace Zachariah Mar Nicholovos, attended by over one thousand faithful. Rev. Fr. Shibu Venad Mathai was appointed Vicar that same year, continuing to lead our vibrant community.

Community Partnership

Since relocating to Bensalem, SGMOC has been an active partner in the local community. Our parish is committed to serving not only our members but also our neighbors, living out the Gospel's call to love and serve all people.

We are proud members of Building a Better Bensalem (B3T), joining with other faith communities and organizations in collaborative service projects, community improvement initiatives, and interfaith fellowship.

Key Initiatives

Bensalem Unity 5K — Organized and conducted in 2016, 2017, and 2019. Our parish community participated to promote wellness and unity among all residents.

Unity Prayer Fellowship — Since 2018, we host and participate in the annual Unity Week celebrations, bringing together faith communities for prayer and fellowship.

Good Samaritan Mission

Love God in Prayer • Serve God by Caring & Sharing

About

The Good Samaritan Mission (GSM) was established in 2016 under the leadership of Rev. Fr. Shibu V. Mathai, with approximately 25+ active members. GSM is a fellowship of elderly and retired faithful of SGMOC, dedicated to caring for those who are physically isolated, emotionally distressed, or spiritually in need.

Ministry Activities: Weekly family visits featuring Bible readings, singing, prayer, and shared meals. GSM also leads Lenten prayers, hosts seasonal gatherings including Onam feasts and Christmas celebrations, and provides financial assistance to those in need.

Community Engagement: GSM actively partners with local organizations on service projects. In 2026, the mission coordinated with the Department of Public Works on a community improvement initiative involving volunteer scouts and skilled laborers, working on landscaping, paving, and facility enhancements with support from local partnerships.
