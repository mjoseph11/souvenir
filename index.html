Life & Ministry

Born on July 3, 1942 in Mallassery, Pathanamthitta, Kerala, India, Mathai Achen felt the call to ministry from a young age — beginning to preach at just eight years old.

He was ordained to the holy priesthood on April 29, 1975, and married Mariamma Abraham the same year on April 14. The following year, he moved from New York to Philadelphia to answer a greater calling — planting the first Malankara Orthodox parish in the region.

In 1976, he established what began as St. Thomas Orthodox Church, celebrating the first liturgy at St. Savior Cathedral Chapel, 38th and Chestnut Streets, Philadelphia. In 1984, the parish was renamed St. Gregorios Malankara Orthodox Church.

Over 35 years of faithful service, he guided the parish through its growth, relocation, and the consecration of its permanent home at 4136 Hulmeville Road, Bensalem on Christmas Day, 2008.

SGMOC Golden Jubilee • 1976–2026

Our Story

Five Decades of Faith • Fellowship • Service

St. Gregorios Malankara Orthodox Church, Bensalem, Pennsylvania stands as one of the earliest parishes established by the Malankara Orthodox Church in the American Diaspora. The parish was founded in 1976 by V. Rev. Kaleeckal Mathai Cor Episcopos, who moved from New York to Philadelphia to serve the growing community of Malayali Orthodox Christians in the greater Philadelphia area. The first Holy Qurbana was celebrated at St. Savior Cathedral Chapel, 38th and Chestnut Streets, Philadelphia.

The parish was originally named St. Thomas Orthodox Church. In 1984, it was renamed St. Gregorios Malankara Orthodox Church — in honor of St. Geevarghese Gregorios of Parumala, the first Indian saint of the Orthodox Church, whose intercessory prayers have guided the parish ever since.

In 1995, the parish moved into its own church building at 7721–31 Torresdale Avenue, Philadelphia, PA 19136 — a milestone moment that gave the congregation a permanent spiritual home after years of borrowed spaces.

On Christmas Day, December 25, 2008, the parish relocated to its current and final home at 4136 Hulmeville Road, Bensalem, PA 19020. The church was formally consecrated in May 2009 by H.G. Mathews Mar Barnabas, Diocesan Metropolitan.

In June 2011, Rev. Fr. Shibu Venad Mathai was appointed Vicar, following the founder's relocation to Kerala after 35 years of dedicated service. Under his leadership, the parish has continued to grow in faith, fellowship, and outreach — nurturing new ministries and welcoming new generations into the Orthodox faith.

Today, SGMOC is a vibrant congregation proudly carrying forward the apostolic tradition — offering the Holy Qurbana in the West Syriac rite, and reaching out with love to all in need. As we celebrate fifty years of God's faithfulness, we give glory to God for every soul that has been a part of this holy parish.

Marth Mariam Vanitha Samajam

Pray • Act • Illumine

About

The Marth Mariam Vanitha Samajam (MMVS) is the historic women's organization of the Malankara Orthodox Church, established in 1920. Recognizing St. Mary as its patron saint, the MMVS operates under the inspiring motto "Pray, Act and Illumine."

The SGMOC unit conducts regular fellowship meetings, Bible studies, and participates in diocesan leadership training, retreats, conferences, and Bible quiz competitions. The unit also supports charitable projects including women's causes in Kerala.

Good Samaritan Mission

Love God in Prayer • Serve God by Caring & Sharing

About

The Good Samaritan Mission (GSM) was established in 2016 under the leadership of Rev. Fr. Shibu V. Mathai, with approximately 25 active members. GSM is a fellowship of elderly and retired faithful of SGMOC, dedicated to caring for those who are physically isolated, emotionally distressed, or spiritually in need.

The ministry conducts weekly family visits featuring Bible readings, singing, prayer, and shared meals. GSM also leads Lenten prayers, hosts seasonal gatherings including Onam feasts and Christmas celebrations, and provides financial assistance to those in need.
